An International Driving Permit (IDP) is an official document that translates your domestic driver's license into multiple languages. It serves as a supplement to your valid driver's license and allows you to drive legally in over 150 countries worldwide. The IDP is recognized under the 1949 Geneva Convention and 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Traffic.

It depends on your destination country and your home country's license. Many countries legally require an IDP for foreign drivers, and most international car rental companies will ask for one. Even if not legally required, an IDP is highly recommended because:

  • • It helps police and officials understand your license
  • • Car rental agencies often require it
  • • Insurance companies may require it for coverage
  • • It can prevent legal complications while traveling

Over 150 countries worldwide accept the International Driving Permit, including:

Europe: All EU countries, UK, Switzerland, Norway, etc.
Asia: Japan, South Korea, Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, etc.
Americas: Canada, Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, Chile, etc.
Africa: South Africa, Morocco, Egypt, Kenya, etc.
Oceania: Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, etc.
Middle East: UAE, Israel, Jordan, etc.
