Frequently Asked Questions

What is an International Driving Permit?

An International Driving Permit (IDP) is a document that translates your existing driver's license into multiple languages. It acts as a supplement to, not a replacement for, your valid driver's license.

Do I need an IDP?

It depends on your destination. Many countries legally require an IDP to drive, and most car rental agencies abroad will ask for one. It's highly recommended for international travel to avoid potential penalties and issues.

How long is my IDP valid for?

An IDP is typically valid for one year from the date of issue, or until your domestic driver's license expires, whichever comes first. Some IDPs issued under different international agreements may be valid for up to three years.

Which countries accept the IDP?

Over 150 countries worldwide accept the International Driving Permit. It is recognized in most of the Americas, Europe, Asia, Africa, and Australia.

How quickly can I get my IDP?

In-person applications at your local issuing office can often be processed in under an hour. Online and mail-in applications can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on the provider and shipping options selected.

What countries is this valid in?

This IDP is valid in over 150 countries that signed the 1949 Geneva Convention and/or 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Traffic, including popular destinations like Europe, Asia, Africa, and South America.
